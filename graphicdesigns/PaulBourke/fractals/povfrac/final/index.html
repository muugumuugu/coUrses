Title: The Old Fractal
Author: Georg Mogk
[POVRay Source]

Panel judge
Initially, this entry made me think of the porcupine puffer fish, because its bones are in the shape of convex hulls, or skeletons, of irregular tetrahedra, which two years ago caused me think, for the first time, about the skeletal structure of regular tetrahedra. The Old Fractal takes this form, or a similar form, and iterates it. These fractal formations and their surroundings conjure notions of skeletal tetrahedron ships at sea, meeting in time warps. The rust on the "old" fractal is so nice. The dodecahedra was an apt choice for the center connection since the tetrahedron sits perfectly inside the dodecahedron, sharing four of its vertices. The 'modern', steel version of the fractal is consistent in this respect, since the tetrahedron also inscribes into a sphere. Overall, I find this entry to be poignant and mesmerizing.

Title: Pernelle
Author: Noe Falzon
[POVRay Source]

Panel judge
I like it that all the spheres run through the same axis. It gives a sense of order. Coloration and shadow draw attention to an imaginary pipeline of expanding spheres that give a sense of vibrational qualities, of reverberations that could potentially generate expansion, perhaps outward, inward, horizontally, whatever growth would be allowed given the constraint of the shared axis, causing the spheres to be confined yet dynamic.
